Root Canal Treatment

Root Canal Treatment

Root Canal Treatment (RCT) is performed when the pulp (nerves and blood vessels) of a tooth becomes infected, with or without pain. The goal is to eliminate infection, preserve the tooth, and restore full function—painlessly.

Procedure

The entire process is painless from start to finish:

  • An X‑ray is taken to assess the tooth.
  • The infected pulp is removed, and the canal is disinfected.
  • When sterile, the canal is sealed with a biocompatible filling material.
  • The crown portion is restored; because the tooth is now brittle, a ceramic crown is recommended to reinforce it.
  • The mechanised procedure is completed in about one hour; antibiotics or painkillers are prescribed only if necessary.

Single‑Sitting RCT

The entire RCT is finished in one visit. An impression is taken for a crown, which is placed at the following appointment.

Multiple‑Sitting RCT

Completed over two visits, 3‑5 days apart. This allows the dentist to confirm canal sterility before final sealing. A crown impression is taken during the second visit, and the crown is fitted at the next.

Laser‑Assisted RCT

Lasers are used adjunctively to achieve superior disinfection and sterilisation of the canals, enhancing treatment success.

Why We Often Prefer Multiple Sittings

  • Allows reassessment of canal sterility
  • Further removal of residual infection if present
  • Ensures long‑term success before permanent sealing

Single‑sitting RCT is ideal in emergencies or when time is limited, and it remains an accepted, reliable option.
