Smile Designing

Cosmetic Orthodontics

Orthodontics is a dental treatment where the dentist reconstructs the entire facial appearance along with functional efficiency, rather than focusing exclusively on teeth.

What all abnormalities can be corrected Orthodontically?

SKELETAL PROBLEMS, DENTAL PROBLEMS, and ORAL HABIT CORRECTIONS

Skeletal Abnormalities and Habit Corrections:

  • Discrepancies in upper and lower jaw bone growth (maxilla and mandible)
  • Tongue thrusting habits in children
  • Mouth breathing during sleep
  • Thumb sucking beyond age 4
  • Chewing inefficiency or difficulty in biting food

Dental Abnormalities:

Any unaesthetic appearance or functional issues due to misaligned teeth can be corrected orthodontically using braces.

What is the right age to start orthodontic treatment?

Skeletal Abnormalities & Habit Corrections:

These issues should be identified and addressed early—preferably during the growth period (up to 12 years in boys and 14 in girls). These corrections are ideally done before beginning braces treatment.

Dental Abnormalities:

Dental braces treatment has no age limit. Many assume orthodontics only means braces, but it encompasses much more.

Smile & Orthodontics

Orthodontic treatment repositions teeth and bones into a desirable alignment to enhance both function and aesthetics.

Types of Braces Used:

  • Ceramic (tooth-coloured) brackets
  • Stainless steel brackets
  • Lingual Orthodontics (brackets fixed on inner side of teeth)
  • Preprosthetic Orthodontics (short-term alignment before smile design)

Preprosthetic orthodontics is a short-term (3 to 6 month) alignment plan to support final smile designing procedures.
